## Useful candidates

### 1. Susan Kare — functional iconography

Potential value:

- icons as functional pictograms rather than decoration;
- concrete and recognizable metaphors;
- legibility at real operating size;
- coherent visual grammar across an icon family;
- evaluation at actual size and enlarged construction view;
- visual personality without sacrificing recognition.

Candidate principle, not yet approved:

`ICONOGRAPHY_IS_A_FUNCTIONAL_LANGUAGE`

> An icon system should communicate objects, states and actions through a coherent visual grammar, remain recognizable at actual use size, and be paired with labels when ambiguity remains.

Required normalization before promotion:

- use Stanford primary interviews and Susan Kare's own published work;
- verify bibliographic metadata and scope;
- distinguish historical evidence, design interpretation and current applicability;
- add tests, anti-patterns, limitations and accessibility boundaries.

Primary gap relation: `GAP-DK-010 — VISUAL_CRAFT`.

### 2. Jakob Nielsen — severity and prioritization

Potential value:

- classify usability findings by severity;
- separate frequency, impact, persistence and recoverability;
- prioritize corrections by user risk and task consequence;
- use heuristic evaluation as detection evidence, not final proof.

Candidate principle, not yet approved:

`USABILITY_FINDINGS_REQUIRE_SEVERITY`

> A usability finding should record scope, estimated frequency, task impact, recoverability and risk before becoming an implementation priority.

Required normalization before promotion:

- use Nielsen Norman Group and original publications only;
- verify severity scales and method boundaries;
- avoid presenting heuristic review as equivalent to representative user testing.

Primary Symphonie value: risk-proportional gates for phases 3, 4 and 6.

### 3. Product focus as explicit exclusion

The useful synthesis from Jobs and Ive is strategic rather than empirical:

- focus requires explicit rejection of features, variants and stimuli;
- simplicity is an outcome of difficult structural work;
- end-to-end coherence matters more than isolated polish;
- product vision and user evidence must constrain each other.

Candidate principle, not yet approved:

`FOCUS_REQUIRES_EXPLICIT_EXCLUSION`

> Product clarity requires documenting what capabilities, variants and stimuli are excluded, as well as what is retained and why.

Boundary:

Claims that market or user research should be rejected are not accepted. The governed synthesis is: research must not replace product vision, and product vision must not replace evidence of use.

### 4. Minimalism versus legitimate density

Accepted synthesis for future tests and anti-patterns:

`MINIMALISM_MUST_NOT_REMOVE_OPERATIONAL_INFORMATION`

Potential checks:

- reduction did not remove necessary labels;
- secondary states remain discoverable;
- hierarchy survives dense data;
- whitespace improves comprehension rather than hiding weak structure;
- expressive restraint does not erase feedback, recovery or navigation.

This candidate extends existing `DKP-002` and `DKP-011`; it does not currently justify a separate canonical principle.

## Methodological findings

1. Primary and secondary sources are mixed without a stable authority taxonomy.
2. Retail listings, Goodreads, Medium, blogs, social media and portfolios appear beside institutional and primary sources.
3. Some links appear to expose potentially unauthorized copies of protected books; these must never enter the LAB source package.
4. Portfolio work is treated at times as evidence of efficacy, although it only proves existence of an artifact.
5. Applicability labels such as high, very high or valid without reservations are subjective inferences without an explicit scoring method.
6. Some purported primary claims are actually journalistic summaries of speeches or interviews.
7. The core-reading list duplicates `The Design of Everyday Things`, reducing real source diversity.
8. The report does not provide normalized source objects, claim objects, limitations, tests, anti-patterns or exact mapping to existing principles.
9. Contemporary designers have weaker bibliographic closure and stronger promotional contamination than the foundational sources.
10. No submitted claim creates a legal, normative, runtime or integration requirement.
