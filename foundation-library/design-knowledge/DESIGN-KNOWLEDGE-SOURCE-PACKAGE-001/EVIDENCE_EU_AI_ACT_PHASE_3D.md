## Accepted findings

1. NIST AI RMF 1.0 is a voluntary government risk-management framework, not binding law or a UI specification.
2. NIST AI 600-1 is a final 2024 Generative AI Profile, not a draft, and does not require a universal confidence score.
3. COGA Usable is an informative W3C Working Group Note/supplemental guidance, not normative WCAG conformance.
4. Cognitive Accessibility Roadmap and Gap Analysis is historical/foundational Working Draft context, not a standard.
5. Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 is primary EU law. Articles 13, 14, 15, 50 and 113 were normalized.
6. Article 13 addresses high-risk-system transparency and instructions for deployers; Article 50 addresses contextual disclosure to exposed persons/public. They are not equivalent.
7. Articles 13 and 15 do not require a numerical confidence score or accuracy display on every output.
8. Article 14 supports proportionate human-oversight capabilities, including disregard/reversal and safe intervention or stop where applicable.
9. Four corrigenda are registered: `R(01)` through `R(04)`. Article 50 received language-specific corrections without a material change to the reviewed obligation.
10. Digital Omnibus procedure `2025/0359(COD)` had an adopted final text, signed 2026-07-08, but no located OJ publication, final act number, CELEX or ELI at cutoff. Status: `ADOPTED_NOT_YET_PUBLISHED`; no present legal effect.

## Reversed or rejected claims

- Universal numerical confidence score for final users: `FALSE`.
- Articles 13 and 50 impose the same transparency obligation: `FALSE`.
- Article 14 requires confirmation before every action: `FALSE`.
- Article 15 requires displaying accuracy on every output: `FALSE`.
- Article 50 requires labeling every AI-generated text without exceptions: `FALSE`.
- Unpublished Omnibus dates may be applied early: `FALSE`.
